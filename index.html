How It Plays

One Ship. One Life.
Infinite Ways to Die.

You pilot a lone fighter through an endless asteroid field. Arrow keys or WASD to move, Space to fire. Each asteroid earns 100 points. One hit through your hull ends the run — unless you have a shield. Learn the rhythm fast. The void doesn't wait.

Every thousand points, the pressure escalates. At 2,000 the asteroid density doubles. At 3,000 they grow armored cores — two shots to crack. At 4,000 a space warp fires: double cannon damage for the stretch ahead. The milestones keep stacking. None of them relent.

"Derelict vessels drift through the sector at 5,000 · 7,000 · 9,000 points. Shoot them before they pass — each one carries a shield worth 1, 2, or 4 hits. Miss them all and you're flying naked into the boss fight."

At 6,000 points, ECHO-1 arrives — your AI wingmate. She auto-fires on asteroids, covers your flanks, and brings 50 HP of her own. At 8,000, a hostile rocket appears: 10 HP, actively targeting ECHO-1. Destroy it for a shield drop. Let it live and she won't make the boss fight.

At 10,000 points, a named dreadnought descends — 100+ HP, dual cannons, a unique attack pattern. At 50% health it enters Phase 2. Kill it and the sector tears open. Choose one of 9 upgrades. Warp deeper. The next boss is harder, faster, and already has a name for you. The void never ends.
