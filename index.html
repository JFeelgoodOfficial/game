Mission Briefing

The Last Transmission
From Fleet Command

Three days ago, the asteroid mining corridor in Sector NOVA-7 went dark. Forty-two ships, six thousand crew — silenced in under an hour. Fleet Command scrambled every available pilot. You are the only one who made it through.

The rocks aren't just rocks anymore. Something out there is driving them — accelerating them, steering them toward your hull. At first they're small, easy to dodge. Then they grow. Then they start taking more than one shot to crack open. Then everything gets worse.

"At 4,000 points Commander Voss's ship passed through an uncharted warp pocket. Hull was changed. Weapons were changed. Whatever came out the other side — it wasn't the same ship that went in. But it was better." — Recovered flight log fragment

At some point you'll hear a second engine beside you. Pilot designation: ECHO-1. Your ally. She's fast, she shoots clean, and she's got your back against the enemy rocket hunting you from the top of the sector. Together you're unstoppable.

Until you're not. At 10,000 points ECHO-1's transponder goes dark and she turns her guns on you. You trained together. You flew together. Now only one of you leaves NOVA-7.

And even if you survive the betrayal — the Dreadnought-class warship GOLIATH awaits. Triple your size. Two cannons. Ten direct hits to bring down. The thing that weaponized the asteroids. The thing that killed your fleet. It ends here.

Destroy GOLIATH, and the coordinates to Elysian-4 unlock — a green planet far beyond the reach of war, where you can finally rest.
